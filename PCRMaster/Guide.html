PCR Master User Guide

PCR Master is a comprehensive tool for sequence analysis, PCR volume calculation, and additional PCR utilities. This guide details how to use each feature available in the application.

Features

1. Sequence Analysis

Description: Analyzes DNA, RNA, and protein sequences to obtain molecular mass, GC content, melting temperature, complementary sequence, and dimer count.

Steps:

  • Select the sequence type (DNA, RNA, Protein) from the dropdown menu.
  • Enter the sequence in the text area.
  • Click "Analyze" to view the results.
  • Optionally, you can export the results to CSV or generate a PDF.

Results Included:

  • Molecular Mass
  • GC Content (%)
  • Melting Temperature (Tm)
  • Complementary Sequence
  • Dimer Count

2. Base Composition Chart

Description: Generates a chart showing the base composition of a sequence.

Steps:

  • Enter the sequence in the appropriate field.
  • The chart will be generated automatically.

3. PCR Calculator

Description: Calculates the total volume needed for a PCR reaction.

Steps:

  • Enter the number of samples.
  • Enter the volume of each component (Mastermix, dNTP, Primers, Template DNA, Water).
  • Click "Calculate" to get the total volume.

4. History

Description: Saves and displays a history of performed analyses and calculations.

Steps:

  • Perform any analysis or calculation.
  • The results will be automatically saved in the history.
  • To view the history, go to the "History" tab.
  • You can delete items from the history by clicking the corresponding "Delete" button.

5. Extra PCR Tools

Description: Additional tools for various PCR-related calculations.

a. Calculate PCR Efficiency

Steps:

  • Click "Calculate PCR Efficiency".
  • Enter the initial and final quantity of PCR product, and the number of cycles.
  • The efficiency will be calculated and displayed automatically.

b. Calculate DNA Concentration

Steps:

  • Click "Calculate DNA Concentration".
  • Enter the absorbance at 260 nm and the dilution factor.
  • The concentration will be calculated and displayed automatically.

c. Calculate Primer Tm

Steps:

  • Click "Calculate Primer Tm".
  • Enter the primer sequence.
  • The melting temperature will be calculated and displayed automatically.

d. Detect Restriction Sites

Steps:

  • Click "Detect Restriction Sites".
  • Enter the DNA sequence and the restriction sites (comma separated).
  • The detected sites will be displayed automatically.
